# 📞 Call Center Operations & Sentiment Analysis

## 📊 Project Overview
This project features a dynamic Call Center Dashboard designed to monitor and optimize operational efficiency and customer satisfaction. By analyzing over 32,000 calls throughout October 2020, the dashboard provides a 360-degree view of performance, from high-level KPIs like response time to granular details of individual customer interactions.

The primary objective was to identify bottlenecks in call volume, understand the root causes of customer inquiries, and measure the overall sentiment of the customer base to improve service delivery.

## 🚀 Key Dashboard Features
- Operational KPIs: Real-time tracking of Total Calls (32,941), Total Call Duration (13,737 hours), and an Average Call Duration of 25 minutes.
- Service Level Monitoring: A clear visualization of the 75% Response Time against SLAs to ensure accountability.
- Sentiment Analysis: A breakdown of customer emotions, revealing a significant volume of "Negative" and "Neutral" feedback that requires strategic intervention.
- Channel Distribution: Insights into how customers reach out, with the Call-Center (32.3%) and Chatbot (25.06%) being the primary touchpoints.
- Geographic & Reason Mapping: A treemap and map visualization identifying Billing Questions as the top call reason and Los Angeles as the highest volume city (41.69%).

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Power BI: Leveraged for the final UI/UX design, interactive filtering (by Date, City, and Channel), and the creation of the executive "Home" and "Table" views.
- Power Query: Used for the ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) process—cleaning messy datasets, standardizing sentiment labels, and merging geographic data for the map visualizations.
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Utilized to create custom measures for Response Time %, Average Call Duration, and Sentiment distribution.

## Business Insights & Recommendations
- The "Sentiment Gap": With over 11,000 "Negative" sentiments recorded, there is a clear need for enhanced agent training, specifically regarding Billing Questions, which are the leading cause of calls.
- Staffing Optimization: Call volume peaks on Thursdays and Fridays (over 5.5K daily calls). Staffing levels should be adjusted during these peaks to improve the 75% response rate.
- Regional Dominance: Los Angeles and Baltimore account for the vast majority of call volume. Targeted local customer service improvements in these hubs could significantly impact overall performance.
- Channel Efficiency: Since Chatbots handle 25% of the volume, further investing in AI-driven resolutions for billing could significantly reduce the load on human agents.
